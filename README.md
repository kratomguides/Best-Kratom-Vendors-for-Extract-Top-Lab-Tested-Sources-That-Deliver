## Understanding Kratom Extract Types and Formats

### Liquid Extracts vs. Extract Powder

Liquid kratom extracts are produced by boiling down concentrated kratom leaf tea to a potent liquid. The final alkaloid concentration depends on starting material quality, leaf-to-liquid ratio, and process control. Quality varies enormously based on all three variables.

Extract powder is produced by taking liquid extract to dryness — creating a concentrated powder that can be used directly, encapsulated, or blended with standard powder. Extract powder allows more precise per-serving dosing than liquid formats and has superior shelf stability.

Both formats require the same core quality documentation. Finished product testing. Milligram alkaloid disclosure per serving. Batch-specific COA. The format preference is a user choice. The documentation requirements are non-negotiable regardless of format.

### Enhanced Leaf vs. Full-Spectrum Extract

Enhanced kratom leaf products combine standard kratom powder with added concentrated extract to boost total alkaloid content. The enhancement level is expressed as a multiplier — "5x enhanced" indicates five times the baseline alkaloid content of standard leaf.

Full-spectrum extracts preserve the complete alkaloid profile of the source leaf including minor alkaloids. Isolate extracts focus on mitragynine concentration alone. Full-spectrum products preserve the secondary alkaloid interactions that contribute to the complete character experienced users associate with quality extract products.

The best documentation for any extract type includes both primary and secondary alkaloid reporting. That reporting is what confirms whether the extraction process preserved or destroyed the broader alkaloid profile.

### Understanding Extract Potency Claims

The "10x" or "50x" potency labeling common in the extract market is frequently misleading. These multipliers are only meaningful when the baseline is defined. 10x compared to what? Standard leaf? A specific mitragynine percentage?

Legitimate extract vendors disclose actual mitragynine milligrams per serving rather than relying on multiplier claims. A product disclosing 45mg mitragynine per serving is providing verifiable information. A product claiming "50x potency" without a corresponding milligram disclosure is providing a marketing claim with no verification pathway.

Always prioritize milligram disclosure over multiplier claims when evaluating extract products.
